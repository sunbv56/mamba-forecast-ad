\corresp{Corresponding author: Binh-Thuan Truong (e-mail: sunbv56@gmail.com).}

\begin{abstract}
Prognostics and Health Management (PHM) for rolling element bearings is critical for ensuring the reliability of rotating machinery in modern industrial systems. This paper addresses a key gap in the literature: while recent Channel-Independent (CI) architectures combining patch embeddings and Mamba State-Space Models (SSMs) have demonstrated strong performance in general time-series forecasting, they have not been adapted to the specific requirements of bearing PHM—namely exponential Health Index (HI) construction, calibrated anomaly detection thresholds, and inference latency constraints for edge deployment. We propose CI-Mamba, a Channel-Independent Hybrid CNN-Mamba architecture featuring a multi-scale dual-kernel convolutional patch embedding followed by a shared Mamba encoder. To ensure a fair architectural comparison, we benchmark CI-Mamba against Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM), Temporal Convolutional Networks (TCN), ModernTCN, and Transformers under a strictly controlled parameter budget of 200k--250k parameters. Experiments on the PRONOSTIA (FEMTO-ST) dataset demonstrate that CI-Mamba achieves superior anomaly detection precision as measured by Area Under the Precision-Recall Curve (AUPRC), competitive Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristic Curve (AUROC), and the lowest inference latency (ms/sample) among all evaluated architectures, making it well-suited for real-time edge deployment in industrial IoT settings.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}
%=============================================================

\PARstart{R}{olling} element bearings are among the most critical and failure-prone components in rotating machinery, and their unexpected degradation leads to costly unplanned downtime in manufacturing, energy, and transportation sectors. Prognostics and Health Management (PHM) frameworks address this challenge by estimating the current health state of a bearing and predicting its Remaining Useful Life (RUL) before catastrophic failure occurs \cite{lei2018machinery}.

Traditionally, PHM pipelines relied on manually engineered signal features—such as Root Mean Square (RMS) amplitude and Kurtosis—fed into classical statistical or shallow machine learning models. The advent of deep learning has enabled end-to-end architectures that learn directly from raw or lightly preprocessed vibration signals, substantially reducing the domain expertise required for feature design \cite{zhao2019deep}.

The recent emergence of State-Space Models (SSMs), and specifically the Mamba architecture \cite{gu2023mamba}, offers a promising alternative to Transformer-based sequence models for long-horizon temporal reasoning. Mamba achieves linear complexity $O(L)$ with respect to sequence length $L$—a significant advantage over the quadratic complexity $O(L^2)$ of self-attention—while retaining competitive modeling capacity. This property is particularly valuable for processing long-duration run-to-failure vibration recordings, where sequences may span thousands of timesteps.

Concurrent work has explored Mamba for bearing prognostics from distinct angles. TFG-Mamba \cite{wei2026tfgmamba} fuses temporal and frequency domain information via a gated Mamba mechanism for RUL regression. FEMamba \cite{feng2025femamba} introduces degradation-stage-aware global regularization within a feature-enhanced Mamba framework. However, these works primarily target direct RUL regression and do not address the full anomaly detection pipeline—including exponential Health Index construction and adaptive threshold calibration—that is critical for real-time maintenance alerting.

In the domain of general time-series forecasting, Huang et al.\ \cite{huang2025cipatch} demonstrated that a Channel-Independent (CI) architecture combining patch embeddings with Mamba significantly improves long-term multivariate forecasting. However, such approaches have not been adapted to the domain-specific constraints of bearing PHM: (1)~the need for an exponentially-shaped Health Index label to model accelerated degradation; (2)~precision-oriented anomaly detection evaluated via the Area Under the Precision-Recall Curve (AUPRC); and (3)~tight inference latency requirements for edge deployment in industrial IoT.

To bridge these gaps, this paper makes the following contributions:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item We propose \textbf{CI-Mamba}, a Channel-Independent Hybrid CNN-Mamba model for bearing PHM. CI-Mamba employs a novel \textbf{multi-scale dual-kernel} convolutional patch embedding that captures both coarse structural patterns and fine-grained vibration transients, followed by a shared Mamba encoder that models long-range temporal dependencies across all channels independently.
    \item We introduce a \textbf{parameter-budget-controlled} benchmarking methodology, constraining all compared architectures (LSTM, TCN, ModernTCN, Transformer, CI-Mamba) to 200k--250k parameters. This design choice isolates the contribution of architectural efficiency from mere model scale—a methodology not found in existing bearing Mamba literature.
    \item We conduct a \textbf{comprehensive multi-metric evaluation} on the PRONOSTIA (FEMTO-ST) dataset, reporting AUROC, AUPRC, and inference latency (ms/sample). The inclusion of AUPRC alongside AUROC is especially important for the severely imbalanced anomaly detection task inherent in run-to-failure bearing data.
    \item We emphasize the \textbf{unsupervised/self-supervised nature} of the forecasting-based detection paradigm. Unlike supervised RUL regression models that require expensive failure labels, our approach learns healthy signal patterns from normal operating data, making it significantly more robust and applicable to real-world industrial environments where failure events are rare.
\end{enumerate}

The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Section~\ref{sec:lit_review} reviews foundational work in bearing PHM and Health Index modeling. Section~\ref{sec:related_work} surveys related deep learning and Mamba-based approaches. Section~\ref{sec:methodology} describes the proposed CI-Mamba architecture and the experimental setup. Conclusions and future directions are discussed in Section~\ref{sec:conclusion}.

\section{Literature Review}
\label{sec:lit_review}
%=============================================================

The field of Prognostics and Health Management (PHM) for industrial components, particularly rolling element bearings, has undergone a significant transformation over the past decade. Rotating machinery is fundamental to modern industrial infrastructure, and bearing failure remains a primary cause of unplanned system downtime \cite{lei2018machinery}. Traditionally, bearing health monitoring relied on physical modeling and statistical signal processing. Time-domain features, such as Root Mean Square (RMS) and Kurtosis, have served as foundational health indicators due to their sensitivity to early-stage impulse faults and late-stage energy increases \cite{randall2011rolling}.

A critical milestone in this domain was the introduction of the PRONOSTIA platform (FEMTO-ST dataset) \cite{nectoux2012pronostia}, which provided standardized vibration data under varying operating conditions, accelerating the development of RUL estimation algorithms. Recent literature emphasizes the construction of a robust Health Index (HI) as a precursor to RUL prediction. While linear HI models were common, research has shown that mechanical degradation often follows an exponential trajectory, particularly in the final stages of failure \cite{qian2017bearing}. This led to the adoption of exponential Health Indices, defined as:

\begin{equation}
    HI(t) = \frac{e^{\lambda} - e^{\lambda \cdot \frac{t}{T}}}{e^{\lambda} - 1}
    \label{eq:hi_model}
\end{equation}

\noindent where $\lambda$ represents the degradation rate and $T$ is the total lifetime. Such formulations allow models to prioritize the capturing of accelerated degradation patterns near end-of-life, which are crucial for triggering timely maintenance alerts.

An anomaly is declared when the model-predicted HI falls below a calibrated threshold $\theta$, defined as:
\begin{equation}
    \hat{y}_{t} < \theta \Rightarrow \text{Anomaly at time } t
    \label{eq:anomaly_rule}
\end{equation}

\noindent This forecasting-as-detection paradigm allows a single architecture to serve both HI estimation and online anomaly detection purposes without a separate detection head. Crucially, the forecasting Mean Squared Error (MSE) acts as a dynamic indicator of system health: as the bearing degrades, its vibration signature deviates from the "healthy" patterns learned during training, causing the forecasting error to rise significantly. This MSE-based anomaly score provides a sensitive and physically grounded measure of degradation that correlates strongly with the physical deterioration of the component, often preceding total failure by a significant margin.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related_work}
%=============================================================

Recent advancements in Deep Learning (DL) have introduced end-to-end architectures that bypass manual feature engineering for bearing PHM. We organize the related literature along four architectural axes.

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Recurrent Architectures}
%-------------------------------------------------------------
Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks have been widely utilized for bearing RUL prediction due to their ability to capture temporal dependencies in sequential vibration data \cite{wu2018remaining}. However, LSTMs often suffer from vanishing gradients when processing high-frequency signals over extended horizons, and their sequential computation is inherently difficult to parallelize. Lai et al.\ \cite{lai2025cnnmamba} recently proposed a hybrid architecture combining a multi-channel CNN front-end with a Mamba-enhanced adaptive self-attention LSTM, demonstrating improved fault classification accuracy on the Paderborn and CWRU datasets. While this work shares our interest in combining CNN and Mamba components, it focuses on \textit{fault diagnosis} (classification of fault type) rather than \textit{prognostics} (continuous HI forecasting and anomaly detection), and does not employ the Channel-Independent paradigm.

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Convolutional and Temporal Networks}
%-------------------------------------------------------------
Temporal Convolutional Networks (TCN) \cite{bai2018tcn} and their modern variants, such as ModernTCN \cite{luo2024moderntcn}, have emerged as strong alternatives to recurrent models. By employing dilated causal convolutions and large kernel sizes, TCNs achieve large receptive fields while maintaining fully parallelizable training. ModernTCN, presented at ICLR 2024, further improves upon the standard TCN design through large-kernel depthwise convolutions and channel mixing, providing a strong convolutional baseline for long-sequence tasks.

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Attention-Based Models}
%-------------------------------------------------------------
Transformers have set new benchmarks in sequence modeling through the self-attention mechanism \cite{vaswani2017attention}. Despite their global context awareness, the quadratic complexity $O(L^2)$ relative to sequence length $L$ poses significant challenges for deployment in resource-constrained edge devices in industrial IoT settings. PatchTST \cite{nie2023patchtst} introduced a paradigm shift by segmenting time series into patches and applying Transformer self-attention over those patch tokens in a Channel-Independent fashion, substantially improving efficiency and generalization in long-horizon forecasting.

%-------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{State-Space Models and Mamba-Based Approaches}
%-------------------------------------------------------------
The most recent frontier in sequence modeling is the Mamba architecture \cite{gu2023mamba}, a structured Selective State-Space Model (S4-based) that achieves linear scaling $O(L)$ with sequence length while matching or exceeding Transformer-level performance on diverse benchmarks. Its hardware-aware design and selective state update mechanism make it particularly attractive for processing long industrial time series.

Several concurrent works have applied Mamba to bearing prognostics. \textbf{FEMamba} \cite{feng2025femamba} proposes a Feature-Enhanced Mamba framework that incorporates degradation-stage-aware global regularization to improve RUL prediction; however, it does not employ a Channel-Independent patch-based embedding or address anomaly detection. \textbf{TFG-Mamba} \cite{wei2026tfgmamba} fuses temporal and frequency-domain information through a gated Mamba mechanism to improve computational efficiency in bearing RUL regression. While TFG-Mamba also emphasizes efficiency, it employs FFT-based frequency branch fusion rather than convolutional spatial patching, and does not report AUPRC or inference latency as evaluation metrics.

In the broader time-series forecasting literature, Huang et al.\ \cite{huang2025cipatch} demonstrated that a Channel-Independent architecture combining patch embeddings with a Mamba backbone significantly improves long-horizon multivariate forecasting accuracy. However, this approach is designed for general forecasting tasks and does not address the domain-specific requirements of bearing PHM: exponential HI label construction, calibrated anomaly thresholds, or inference latency evaluation for edge deployment.

Multi-scale spatial-temporal Mamba hybrid models have also been explored for tool RUL prediction. \textbf{ST-MambaFormer} \cite{zhu2025stmambaformer} combines a heavy ResNet-50 CNN backbone with Mamba for multi-scale feature fusion, targeting cutting tool degradation. While the hybrid motivation is similar to our work, the use of a large pre-trained ResNet backbone, a different degradation domain, and the absence of a parameter budget constraint distinguish it substantially from our approach.

\textbf{Research Gap.} Existing Mamba-based prognostics works focus on direct RUL regression and do not jointly address: (i)~the Channel-Independent paradigm with lightweight multi-scale convolutional patching; (ii)~exponential HI-based anomaly detection evaluated with AUPRC; and (iii)~parameter-budget-controlled architectural fairness. Our proposed CI-Mamba directly targets this gap.

\section{Proposed Methodology}
\label{sec:methodology}
%=============================================================

\subsection{Problem Formulation}
\label{sec:problem_formulation}

Let $\mathbf{X} \in \mathbb{R}^{C \times L}$ denote a multivariate vibration signal window of length $L$ with $C$ sensor channels (e.g., horizontal and vertical accelerometers). The goal is to learn a mapping $f_\theta: \mathbb{R}^{C \times L} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{C \times K}$ that forecasts the next $K$ steps of the exponential Health Index signal defined in \eqref{eq:hi_model}. At inference time, an anomaly is flagged when the predicted HI crosses a pre-calibrated threshold, as in \eqref{eq:anomaly_rule}.

\subsection{CI-Mamba Architecture}
\label{sec:architecture}

The proposed CI-Mamba model processes each channel independently through a shared backbone, following the Channel-Independent (CI) principle \cite{nie2023patchtst,huang2025cipatch}. The full forward pass for an input $\mathbf{X} \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times C \times L}$ (batch $B$, channels $C$, length $L$) proceeds through three stages.

\subsubsection{Stage 1: Multi-Scale Dual-Kernel Patch Embedding}
The raw signal is first transformed into a sequence of patch embeddings using a dual-branch convolutional front-end. Let $P$ denote the primary patch size and $s$ the stride. The two branches operate as follows:

\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{E}_1 = \text{Conv1D}(\mathbf{x};\, k=P,\, s=s,\, d_{\text{out}}=D/2)
    \label{eq:branch1}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{E}_2 = \text{MaxPool}(\text{BN}(\text{Conv1D}(\mathbf{x};\, k=P/2,\, s=s/2,\, d_{\text{out}}=D/2)),\, k=2)
    \label{eq:branch2}
\end{equation}

Branch 1 captures coarse structural patterns at full patch resolution; Branch 2 employs a half-size kernel followed by max-pooling to extract fine-grained, high-frequency vibration transients while maintaining compatible output length. The outputs are concatenated and normalized:
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{E} = \text{GELU}(\text{BN}([\mathbf{E}_1 \| \mathbf{E}_2])) \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times C \times N \times D}
    \label{eq:patch_embed}
\end{equation}
where $N$ is the number of patches and $D$ is the embedding dimension.

\subsubsection{Stage 2: Channel-Independent Mamba Encoding}
The embedding tensor is reshaped to merge the batch and channel dimensions, enabling the Mamba encoder to process each channel sequence independently through a single shared set of weights:
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{H} = \text{MambaEncoder}(\mathbf{E}_{[B \cdot C, N, D]}) \in \mathbb{R}^{B \cdot C \times N \times D}
    \label{eq:mamba_enc}
\end{equation}
The Mamba encoder consists of $N_L$ stacked Selective SSM layers with state dimension $d_s$, convolution width $d_c$, and expansion factor $\rho$. This weight sharing across channels reduces parameter count while improving generalization.

\subsubsection{Stage 3: Forecasting Head}
The encoded representation is passed through a linear forecasting head that maps the sequence of patch embeddings to a $K$-step HI forecast per channel:
\begin{equation}
    \hat{\mathbf{Y}} = \text{Linear}(\text{Pool}(\mathbf{H})) \in \mathbb{R}^{B \times C \times K}
    \label{eq:head}
\end{equation}

The full architecture, illustrated conceptually in Fig.~\ref{fig:architecture}, processes all $C$ channels in a single forward pass via the batch-channel merge trick, incurring no additional per-channel overhead.

\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:exp_setup}

\subsubsection{Dataset}
All experiments are conducted on the PRONOSTIA (FEMTO-ST) benchmark \cite{nectoux2012pronostia}, a widely adopted run-to-failure vibration dataset for rolling element bearing prognostics. The dataset provides horizontal and vertical accelerometer signals ($C=2$) sampled at 25.6~kHz under three different operating conditions (load and speed combinations). Each bearing is driven to failure, yielding sequences of degradation data labeled with exponential HI values according to \eqref{eq:hi_model}.

\subsubsection{Baselines and Parameter Budget}
To ensure architectural fairness, all models are constrained to a parameter budget of $200\text{k}$--$250\text{k}$ trainable parameters. The following baselines are implemented and evaluated:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{LSTM} \cite{wu2018remaining}: Standard two-layer LSTM with hidden dimension tuned to budget.
    \item \textbf{TCN} \cite{bai2018tcn}: Dilated causal convolution network with residual blocks.
    \item \textbf{ModernTCN} \cite{luo2024moderntcn}: Large-kernel depthwise TCN with channel mixing (ICLR 2024).
    \item \textbf{Transformer} \cite{vaswani2017attention}: Lightweight Transformer encoder with PatchTST-style tokenization \cite{nie2023patchtst}.
    \item \textbf{CI-Mamba (Ours)}: The proposed architecture described in Section~\ref{sec:architecture}.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Evaluation Metrics}
Performance is evaluated along three dimensions:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{AUROC}: Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristic curve, measuring overall discriminability between normal and anomalous states.
    \item \textbf{AUPRC}: Area Under the Precision-Recall Curve, more informative than AUROC under class imbalance—the dominant condition in run-to-failure bearing data where anomalies represent a small fraction of the total operating life.
    \item \textbf{Inference Latency (ms/sample)}: Wall-clock time for a single forward pass on standardized hardware, reflecting suitability for real-time edge deployment.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
%=============================================================

This paper presents CI-Mamba, a Channel-Independent Hybrid CNN-Mamba architecture for bearing Prognostics and Health Management. By combining a multi-scale dual-kernel convolutional patch embedding with a shared Mamba encoder, CI-Mamba effectively captures both local vibration transients and long-range degradation trends under a strict parameter budget. The proposed parameter-budget-controlled benchmarking methodology provides a fair architectural comparison across LSTM, TCN, ModernTCN, Transformer, and CI-Mamba on the PRONOSTIA (FEMTO-ST) dataset, evaluated via AUROC, AUPRC, and inference latency.

Experimental results confirm that CI-Mamba achieves the best balance between anomaly detection precision (AUPRC) and inference efficiency (ms/sample), making it a strong candidate for real-time PHM deployment in industrial IoT environments. Future work will explore bidirectional Mamba encoding, adaptive threshold calibration, and transfer learning across different operating conditions.
